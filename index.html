Frequently Asked Questions

What are valid reasons to protest?

You can protest your appraisal if you believe:

  • The appraised (market) value is higher than your property's actual market value.
  • Your property was appraised unequally compared to similar neighboring properties.
  • There are errors in the appraisal records — such as incorrect size, condition, or ownership information.
What evidence should I bring?

Strong evidence makes a stronger case. Bring as much of the following as you can:

  • The protest report you received from us — it includes comparable property data and a suggested fair value.
  • Photos of your property showing needed repairs, damage, or issues affecting value.
  • Repair receipts, contractor estimates, or inspection reports.
  • An independent appraisal or insurance valuation if available.
  • Documentation of any errors in the appraisal record (e.g., incorrect square footage).
Which counties are currently covered?

We currently support Brazos County (College Station, Bryan, and surrounding areas) for the 2026 tax year.

We plan to expand to additional Texas counties in the future. If you would like us to cover your county, please contact us at support@lonestartaxsave.com.

How do I submit my appeal?

You have two options:

  • By mail: Complete the Property Owner's Notice of Protest form you received for 2026. Use the information in your protest report to fill out Section 4, then mail it along with any supporting evidence to:
    Brazos CAD — 4051 Pendleton Dr., Bryan, TX 77802-2465
  • Online: File your protest directly at brazoscad.org through the Appeals Portal.
